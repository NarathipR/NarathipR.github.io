Mask R-CNN for Pulmonary Embolism Detection

The study explores the use of convolutional neural networks (CNN), specifically ResNet50 and ResNet101 architectures within Mask R-CNN, for identifying blood clots in lung arteries using CT images. While ResNet101 shows slightly better average precision than ResNet50, it requires an additional hour of training time, indicating that deeper architectures offer marginal performance gains with increased computational cost.

Introduction

The CDC reports that annually, 900,000 Americans suffer from blood clot conditions, resulting in 100,000 deaths and $10 billion in costs. Known as Pulmonary Embolism (PE), this condition involves blood clots in pulmonary arteries. Traditional diagnosis relies on blood tests or medical imaging, but shortages of specialized personnel necessitate image classification to alleviate doctors' workload. Medical imaging techniques like X-ray, CT, Ultrasound, and MRI are crucial for diagnosis, with early detection potentially reducing the death rate to 3-8%. Implementing convolutional neural networks (CNN) for image classification, particularly in identifying blood clots in lung arteries using CT images, is recommended. The study compares ResNet50 and ResNet101 architectures within Mask R-CNN to evaluate effectiveness and explore enhancements.

Dataset

The data set is from the public dataset of CT Scan images called FUMPE (Ferdowsi University of Mashhad's PE dataset) [1]. This dataset is collected from 35 patients including 17 men and 18 women aged 24-82 years old. The size of the images is 512 x 512 pixels. The images are divided into three sets: training set, validation set and test set. As the pictures are in DICOM format (Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine), they have to be converted into JPEG format first. Before feeding to the model, the images are augmented by flipping left and right with a probability of 0.5.

Result

The training process takes around six hours for 40 epochs. As training progresses with each epoch, the losses of all cases continue to decrease which means that they are converging. The test set is then used to test the performance of each case. Evaluation indicator in this study is Average Precision (AP) which AP50 and AP75 are focused on. For comparison between ResNet50 and ResNet101, the model with ResNet101 has slightly higher average precision and both models achieve almost AP50 of 80.

Discusion and Conclusion

According to the result in Table.1 , implementation of ResNet50 and ResNet101 do not have a significant difference in average precision. The average precision of the model with ResNet101 is slightly better than the model with ResNet50 by 0.9. However, the training time for ResNet101 is approximately one hour longer than that for ResNet50. It can be concluded that ResNet with more layers contributes to marginally higher performance, but more training time is required. Therefore, the number of layers has little impact on the outcome.
