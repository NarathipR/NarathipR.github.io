U.S. Gas Price Prediction with Long Short-term Memory(LSTM)

Problem Statement

Reliable gas price predictions are essential for effective resource allocation and decision-making across sectors. There is a critical need for accurate forecasting methods to mitigate the impacts of fluctuation of gas prices on stakeholders.

Goal

The goal of this project is to develop a robust and accurate gas price forecasting model using convolutional neural network(CNN).

Dataset

The U.S. Energy Information Administration has documented the Gasoline Price and Exportation data, encompassing 1142 records spanning from November 9, 2001, to September 22, 2023.

Approach

There are three approaches including Polynomial Regression, CNN and RNN.

Result

It was determined that the best CNN model has parameters of [8,8,32] with a learning rate of 0.0006, 4 layers and the Adam optimizer. The SGD and RMSprop optimizers have a higher loss than the Adam optimizer and get the same result with each other with little improvement. The model was then tested with minibatch and it led to a faster convergence to the loss value. The validation loss was 0.45 USD2 and MAE was 0.64 USD. The baseline model's, a 9 degree linear regression, MAE was 0.34 USD which outperformed the deep learning model and is recommended for the dataset. As recurrent neural network(RNN) is good to be used on time series data, the gasoline price can be predicted using previous price information only. The validation loss was 0.0074 USD2 and MAE for test data was 0.1 USD.
